polar decomposition in von Neumann algebras


- Let ℳ be a von Neumann algebra acting on a Hilbert space H and T∈ℳ. If T=V⁢R is the polar decomposition for T with K⁢e⁢r⁢V=K⁢e⁢r⁢R, then both V and R belong to ℳ.

Proof :

  • •

    As ℳ is a C*-algebra (http://planetmath.org/CAlgebra), it is known that R=T*⁢T belongs to ℳ. (proof will be added later)

  • •

    To see that V also belongs to ℳ, by the double commutant theorem, it suffices to show that V belongs to ℳ′′ (the double commutant of ℳ).

    Suppose S∈ℳ′. We intend to prove that V commutes with S.

    For x∈H we have that

    T⁢S⁢x=S⁢T⁢x=S⁢V⁢R⁢x

    and

    T⁢S⁢x=V⁢R⁢S⁢x=V⁢S⁢R⁢x

    So S⁢V and V⁢S agree on R⁢a⁢n⁢R¯.

    As R is self-adjoint, R⁢a⁢n⁢R¯⟂=K⁢e⁢r⁢R, and so it remains to show that S⁢V and V⁢S agree on K⁢e⁢r⁢R. Recall that, by hypothesis, K⁢e⁢r⁢R=K⁢e⁢r⁢V.

    Let x∈K⁢e⁢r⁢R. We have that R⁢S⁢x=S⁢R⁢x=0 and therefore

    S⁢(K⁢e⁢r⁢R)⊆K⁢e⁢r⁢R=K⁢e⁢r⁢V

    and so we can conclude that V⁢S is identically zero in K⁢e⁢r⁢R.

    Clearly S⁢V is also identically zero on K⁢e⁢r⁢R=K⁢e⁢r⁢V.

    Thus V⁢S and S⁢V agree on K⁢e⁢r⁢R. Therefore S⁢V=V⁢S and so V∈ℳ′′=ℳ⁢□
