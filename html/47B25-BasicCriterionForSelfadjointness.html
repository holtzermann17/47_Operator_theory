basic criterion for self-adjointness


Let A:D⁢(A)⊂ℋ→ℋ be a symmetric operator on a Hilbert space. The following are equivalent:

  1. 1.

    A=A* (i.e A is self-adjoint);

  2. 2.

    Ker⁡(A*±i)={0} and A is closed;

  3. 3.

    Ran⁡(A±i)=ℋ.

Remark: A+λ represents the operator A+λ⁢I:D⁢(A)⊂ℋ→ℋ, and Ker and Ran stand for kernel and range, respectively.

A similar version for essential self-adjointness is an easy corollary of the above. The following are equivalent:

  1. 1.

    A¯=A* (i.e. A is essentially self-adjoint);

  2. 2.

    Ker⁡(A*±i)={0};

  3. 3.

    Ran⁡(A±i) is dense in ℋ.
