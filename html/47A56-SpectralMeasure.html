5 Remarks

The second example we gave above, of a spectral measure associated with a normal operator, is in some sense the general case: all spectral projections in â„‚ supported in a compact set arise from a normal operator. Thus, to any such spectral projection we can associate a normal operator and vice-versa. This interplay between spectral projections and normal operators is deeply explored in some versions of the spectral theorem.
