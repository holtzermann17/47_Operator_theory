proof of Taylor’s formula for matrix functions


Theorem.

Let p be a polynomial and suppose A and B are squared matrices of the same size, then p⁢(A+B)=∑k=0n1k!⁢p(k)⁢(A)⁢Bk where n=deg⁡(p).

Proof.

Since p is a polynomial, we can apply the Taylor expansion:

p⁢(x)=∑k=0n1k!⁢p(k)⁢(x0)⁢(x-x0)k

where n=deg⁡(p). Now let x=𝐀+𝐁 and x0=𝐀.

The Taylor expansion can be checked as follows: let p⁢(x)=∑k=0nak⁢xk for coefficients ak (note that this coefficients can be taken from the space of square matrices defined over a field). We define the formal derivative of this polynomial as p(1)⁢(x)=d⁢pd⁢x=∑k=1nak⁢k⁢xk-1 and we define p(k)=d⁢p(k-1)d⁢x.

Then p(k)⁢(x)=∑i=knai⁢i!(i-k)!⁢xi-k and we have 1k!⁢p(k)⁢(x0)=∑i=knai⁢i!(i-k)!⁢k!⁢(x0)i-k. Now consider

∑k=0n1k!⁢p(k)⁢(x0)⁢(x-x0)k=∑k=0n(∑i=knai⁢i!(i-k)!⁢k!⁢(x0)i-k⁢(x-x0)k)
= ∑i=0nai⁢(x0)i+∑i=1nai⁢i⁢(x0)i-1⁢(x-x0)+…+∑i=jnai⁢i!(i-j)!⁢j!⁢(x0)i-j⁢(x-x0)j+…+an⁢(x-x0)n
= a0+a1⁢(x)+…+ai⁢(∑j=0ii!(i-j)!⁢j!⁢(x0)i-j⁢(x-x0)j)+…+an⁢(∑j=0nn!(n-j)!⁢j!⁢(x0)n-j⁢(x-x0)j)
= ∑k=0nak⁢xi=p⁢(x)

since ∑j=0ii!(i-j)!⁢j!⁢(x0)i-j⁢(x-x0)j=(x)i. ∎
