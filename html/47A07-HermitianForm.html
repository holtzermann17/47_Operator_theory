Hermitian form


A sesquilinear form over a pair of complex vector spaces (V,W) is a function B:V×W→ℂ satisfying the following properties:

  1. 1.

    B⁢(𝐯1+𝐯2,𝐰)=B⁢(𝐯1,𝐰)+B⁢(𝐯2,𝐰)

  2. 2.

    B⁢(𝐯,𝐰1+𝐰2)=B⁢(𝐯,𝐰1)+B⁢(𝐯,𝐰2)

  3. 3.

    B⁢(c⁢𝐯,d⁢𝐰)=c⁢B⁢(𝐯,𝐰)⁢d¯

for all 𝐯,𝐯1,𝐯2∈V, 𝐰,𝐰1,𝐰2∈W, and c,d∈ℂ. The vector spaces V and W are often identical, although the definition does not require them to be the same vector space.

A sesquilinear form B:V×V→ℂ over a single vector space V is called a Hermitian form if it is complex conjugate symmetric: namely, if B⁢(𝐯1,𝐯2)=B⁢(𝐯2,𝐯1)¯.

An inner product over a complex vector space is a positive definite Hermitian form.
